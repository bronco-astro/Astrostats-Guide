Chapter 5 — Nonparametric Statistics

Nonparametric methods are used when data do not follow a known probability distribution. Instead of assuming a specific functional form, these methods rely more directly on the data itself.

In astronomy, data often violate ideal assumptions. Measurements may be noisy, skewed, censored, or contain outliers. Nonparametric methods are useful in these situations because they make fewer assumptions.

Why nonparametric methods are needed

Many statistical methods assume data follow a specific distribution, such as the normal distribution. In real astronomical data, this assumption is often not valid.

Nonparametric methods avoid strong assumptions about distribution shape. They are more flexible and often more robust when working with real observations.

Ranks instead of values

A key idea in nonparametric statistics is the use of ranks instead of actual values.

Data points are ordered from smallest to largest, and analysis is performed using their positions in the ordered list. This reduces sensitivity to extreme values and measurement errors.

Empirical distribution function

The empirical distribution function describes the distribution of observed data directly.

It increases step by step as data values increase. Each step corresponds to one observation. This function provides a nonparametric estimate of the cumulative distribution.

The empirical distribution function converges to the true distribution as more data are collected.

Sample quantiles

Quantiles describe positions within a dataset. The median divides the data into two equal parts. Other quantiles divide the data into more parts.

Quantiles are nonparametric summaries because they do not depend on any assumed distribution. They are useful for skewed data and data with outliers.

The sign test

The sign test is a simple nonparametric method for testing whether a distribution is centered around a specific value.

Only the direction of deviation matters, not the size. This makes the test robust but less sensitive than parametric alternatives.

The Wilcoxon tests

Wilcoxon tests use ranks to compare data sets.

The Wilcoxon signed-rank test is used for paired data. The Wilcoxon rank-sum test is used to compare two independent samples.

Correlation without assuming linearity

Nonparametric correlation measures describe relationships between variables without assuming a linear relationship.

Rank-based correlation measures detect monotonic relationships and are less sensitive to outliers.

Two-sample comparisons

Nonparametric methods are often used to compare two samples when their distributions are unknown or unequal.

These methods test whether two samples come from the same underlying distribution without specifying its shape.

Strengths and limitations

Nonparametric methods are robust and flexible. They work well with small sample sizes and non-normal data.

However, they may discard some information and can be less efficient when parametric assumptions are actually valid.
